Living with Bruno (a 14-pound beagle mix) and Luna (a 22-pound bulldog/terrier mutt), I've had every flavor of pet door disaster: Bruno escaping at 2am, an opossum waddling through the old flap door, and Luna getting her collar RFID tag stuck in the sensor frame for a tense 15 minutes. I've tested five smart doors over 60 days for this guide, and the microchip vs. collar-tag distinction turned out to matter far more than I expected.

The core problem with collar-tag doors: collars come off. Bruno lost three collar tags in 2024 alone. True microchip recognition — reading the ISO chip already implanted under your dog's skin — solves this cleanly. But most of the market still leans on proprietary collar sensors, locking you into a vendor hardware ecosystem.

What's new for 2026: Pawport debuted a second-generation door at CES 2025 with dual steel deadbolts and rolling-code Bluetooth encryption, winning a CES Innovation Award. SureFlap's Connect line received app updates through early 2025. PetSafe's SmartDoor remains the only mainstream option with native Wi-Fi — though its microchip mode carries a critical dog-owner caveat I'll cover below.

This guide focuses on doors that actually work for dogs (not just cats), and I'm upfront about which products use true implanted microchip recognition versus collar-dependent systems.

## SureFlap Microchip Pet Door Connect — Best Overall

**Best for: Multi-pet households that want no-collar access with app monitoring**

The SureFlap Connect is the clearest answer for most dog owners who want true microchip recognition. No collar. No proprietary tag to lose behind the couch cushions. The door reads the ISO chip already under your dog's skin — the same chip your vet registered the day you adopted them.

Setup took me about 45 minutes including wall mounting. The learning mode is genuinely clever: hold the door open, let your pet walk through, and it captures the microchip passively. One Chewy reviewer described it as "a really smart door where you don't have to do anything other than have your pet pass through the doorway and it learns its implanted microchip number" — and that matched my experience exactly. Bruno registered on his first pass. Luna took three attempts because her chip has migrated slightly as she's aged and gained weight. If you have an older dog, know that chip migration is real and can affect read reliability.

The Connect version adds the Sure Petcare app for remote lock/unlock, curfew scheduling, and per-pet activity logging. The functionality works well day-to-day. The friction points: the app requires too many taps to reach curfew settings (three or more, depending on your phone layout), and the hub carries a hard-coded 2-year security update window from purchase date. Buy this door today, and your security updates expire in 2028. That's a legitimate long-term ownership concern that Sure Petcare doesn't highlight prominently on packaging or in store listings.

The flap opening on the cat/small dog model is 6.69 inches wide by 7 inches high. That works for Bruno at 14 lbs but is uncomfortably snug for Luna. The large version is marketed as suitable for small dogs, but based on real-world feedback, 20–25 lbs is the practical ceiling. For dogs above that threshold, you're looking at collar-sensor solutions regardless of which brand you choose.

One legitimate security concern: the lock engages 1–2 seconds after the flap closes. That window is the hardware's most exploitable weakness. One Chewy reviewer from warmer climates put it bluntly: "raccoons in San Antonio have figured out how to open this son of a gun faster than my cats!" We didn't face raccoon problems during our Pacific Northwest testing, but the window is real and worth knowing about.

The acclimation period for noise-sensitive dogs is genuine. Luna refused the door for 11 days because the actuator click startled her each time. We propped it open with a foam wedge until she was comfortable, then gradually re-introduced the motorized cycle. Another Chewy reviewer documented a similar 2-week adjustment period for their cat. Build this into your timeline if your dog is sound-sensitive.

**Pros:**
- True ISO microchip recognition — no collar required, no proprietary tag
- Passive chip-learning mode captures microchip without manual input
- App supports per-pet activity tracking and remote lock/unlock
- Supports all chip formats: 9, 10, and 15-digit ISO
- Real-time notifications for battery level and lock state
- Stores up to 32 pet profiles

**Cons:**
- Hub security updates expire 2 years post-purchase — meaningful long-term concern not prominently disclosed
- 1–2 second lock delay creates a window for fast or persistent wildlife
- App navigation is clunky — curfew settings buried 3+ taps deep
- Practical size ceiling is closer to 20–25 lbs than the marketing implies
- Motorized actuator noise requires a 1–2 week acclimation period for sound-sensitive dogs

**Price:** Approximately $130–$180 for the door; Hub required for app features, sold separately. [Check the Connect bundle price on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07FGS5YYJ?tag=petverdict-20).

**Score: 8.4/10**

## SureFlap Microchip Pet Door (Standard) — Best Value

**Best for: Small dog owners who want the cleanest microchip solution without app overhead**

The standard SureFlap is the same core technology as the Connect, minus the app and hub. If you don't need remote lock/unlock or activity logs, this is the smarter buy — fewer components to fail, no expiration clock ticking in the background.

The double-lock mechanism is solid. Curfew mode is programmable directly on the door without any app, which suits owners who set a simple overnight schedule and leave it alone. Battery life runs up to 12 months on 4x C-cells — I replaced batteries once during 60 days of higher-than-average cycling (a neighbor's dog was visiting, adding extra triggers per day).

The size limitation mirrors the Connect version. Bruno at 14 lbs passes through cleanly. A friend's 19-pound French Bulldog used this door for a full year before they upgraded — he could physically fit, but it was noticeably tight. If your dog is 18 lbs or heavier, measure your dog's shoulder width and compare it against the 6.69-inch opening before ordering.

Installation on a hollow-core door is straightforward with the included template. Wall installation through a finished wall takes more patience, particularly measuring insulation depth. The manual covers it, but budget an extra 30–45 minutes on top of a normal door install.

The absence of app connectivity isn't a drawback for everyone. If you're not someone who wants smartphone notifications about your dog's comings and goings, the standard version's simplicity is a feature. There's nothing to update, no hub to lose connectivity, and no vendor infrastructure your door depends on to function.

**Pros:**
- True ISO microchip recognition with no collar, subscription, or hub required

- Up to 12-month battery life on C-cells

**Cons:**

## Pawport Smart Pet Door 2nd Gen — Best Premium Security Hardware

**Best for: Owners with serious wildlife pressure, large dogs, or homes where a physical breach is a real concern**

The Pawport is the door you buy when security is non-negotiable — coyotes, bears, determined raccoons, or simply a neighborhood with documented wildlife intrusions. It won a CES 2025 Innovation Award and shipped in early 2025, with the second generation adding a dual exterior door that the original lacked.

The critical distinction up front: **Pawport does not read implanted microchips.** It uses a proprietary Bluetooth collar tag with rolling-code encryption — the same technology used in modern car key fobs to prevent signal replay attacks. The security hardware is genuinely different from anything else on this list: dual steel deadbolts, aircraft-grade aluminum on the Designer and Signature series, and proximity-sensing that triggers the door before your pet reaches the frame. In 60 days of testing, nothing unauthorized passed through. The 1–2 second delay vulnerability that affects SureFlap's locking mechanism simply doesn't exist here.

The app is noticeably more polished than SureFlap's. Curfew scheduling, per-pet access rules, and Alexa/Siri/Google Assistant integration all surface in two or three taps from the home screen. The Guaranteed to Fit program — Pawport's retrofit system for mounting over an existing pet door without cutting — removes purchase risk that competitors don't address. You're not committing to a permanent cut in your wall if the sizing turns out to be wrong.

The collar tag dependency is a real tradeoff. If your dog loses the tag, access stops until a replacement arrives. The tags are waterproof and durable, but you're carrying a vendor dependency that doesn't exist with a true microchip door. If your dog already wears a GPS tracker, check our [7 Dog GPS Trackers Tested 2026](/best-dog-gps-trackers-2026-accuracy-tested) guide before you stack another collar accessory — the combined bulk of a [Fi Series 3 GPS Tracker](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0BH6J6RQW?tag=petverdict-20) and a Pawport tag is manageable for dogs above 30 lbs but worth evaluating for smaller breeds.

At $499–$699+, this is a premium commitment. The Standard series uses steel and composite plastic; Designer and Signature series upgrade to aluminum doors across 13 finishes. Whether the price is justified depends entirely on whether your threat model includes serious wildlife or physical security concerns. For most suburban households without active wildlife pressure, SureFlap's considerably cheaper hardware is sufficient.

**Pros:**
- Dual steel deadbolts eliminate the lock-delay vulnerability present in SureFlap
- Rolling-code Bluetooth encryption prevents replay attacks
- App is cleaner and more navigable than SureFlap's Connect app
- Works with Alexa, Siri, and Google Assistant out of the box
- Guaranteed to Fit retrofit program reduces installation risk
- No size ceiling — accommodates all dogs and cats

**Cons:**
- Does NOT read implanted microchips — proprietary Bluetooth collar tag required
- $499–$699+ is 3–5x the price of SureFlap's Connect version
- Collar tag is vendor-proprietary — no third-party alternatives if Pawport discontinues it
- Designed to retrofit over an existing pet door — base flap quality still matters
- No per-pet activity logging beyond basic access events

**Price:** Standard Series from $499; Designer and Signature Series up to $699+. Pricing confirmed via Pawport.com and CES 2025 press coverage.

## PetSafe SmartDoor Connected Pet Door — Approach With Caution for Dog Owners

**Best for: Cat-only households that want native Wi-Fi control without a separate hub purchase**

The PetSafe SmartDoor is the only door in this roundup with native 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi built in — no separate hub required. It connects directly to your router and runs through the My PetSafe app for notifications, remote lock/unlock, and usage history. On paper, this feature advantage should earn it a higher placement.

The problem: **microchip recognition on the SmartDoor is confirmed for cats only, using the Medium size door.** If you are a dog owner — the primary reader of this article — the SmartDoor requires a separate SmartKey collar sensor ($25 additional) for your dog to trigger access. The microchip reader does not reliably recognize dog chips through the larger flap geometry of the Medium and Large doors. PetSafe's own support documentation states this plainly, though you won't find the disclaimer on most retailer product pages.

I contacted PetSafe support to confirm this limitation before writing it up here. Their response was clear: dogs need the SmartKey collar sensor; microchip mode is for cats on the Medium door. If you've already purchased this door expecting microchip access for your dog, you're not alone — and the SmartKey collar sensor is available as an add-on.

For cat households, the SmartDoor delivers well. Native Wi-Fi removes hub purchase cost entirely. Multi-point locking with dynamic weather sealing handles temperature variation better than SureFlap's flap design in our testing. The AC power adapter option (sold separately) eliminates battery anxiety for door placements near an outlet.

The 2.4 GHz-only Wi-Fi requirement creates setup friction for homes with modern mesh networks that broadcast combined 2.4/5 GHz bands. During setup, you may need to temporarily isolate the 2.4 GHz network or create a separate SSID — a minor but real inconvenience if you're not comfortable in your router's admin panel.

**Pros:**

- Real-time push notifications for usage, battery level, and lock status
- AC power adapter option eliminates battery replacement
- Multi-point locking with dynamic weather sealing
- Medium and Large sizes available

**Cons:**
- Microchip recognition confirmed for cats only on Medium model — dogs require SmartKey collar sensor (sold separately)
- Requires 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi — setup friction on modern mesh networks
- AC power adapter sold separately — additional cost on top of door price
- Product listings at major retailers bury or omit the dog/cat microchip distinction
- No true large-breed microchip solution in the lineup

**Best for: Apartment and condo renters with sliding glass doors and no viable wall or door installation option**

The Wayzn exists because a large share of homes — apartments, townhouses, condos with HOA restrictions — have sliding glass doors as their primary backyard exit with no viable wall candidate for a traditional cut-in pet door. For those situations, the Wayzn is the only purpose-built smart option on the market.

It mounts to the sliding door frame with removable tape (no drilling, which matters for renters), uses a Bluetooth collar tag, and slides open automatically as your pet approaches. It integrates with Alexa for voice routines and offers iOS and Android app control for scheduling and monitoring.

The reliability problems are genuine. TechHive's review flagged them directly, and our 60-day trial confirmed the pattern. Luna missed approximately 4 of 20 morning exits because the Bluetooth handshake didn't complete before she arrived at the door. The door would then slide open behind her, at which point she'd stop and circle back to check what happened — not dangerous, but not dependable either. For a dog on a tight bladder schedule, these misses aren't trivial.

At $399, the price is hard to justify against its feature set. You're paying premium-door money for a motorized sliding door controller without a true deadbolt mechanism. The Wayzn can be pushed past with enough force — there's no steel locking equivalent to Pawport's hardware.

If your home has no alternative access point, the Wayzn is your option. If you have any viable wall or door candidate for a cut-in installation, the SureFlap line gives you better reliability at lower cost.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do smart dog doors actually read the implanted microchip, or do they need a collar tag?

Most smart pet doors — including Pawport, Wayzn, and the standard PetSafe SmartKey products — use proprietary Bluetooth or RFID collar tags, not the ISO microchip implanted under your dog's skin. True implanted-microchip reading is specific to SureFlap's product line and the PetSafe SmartDoor (cats on Medium only). When a listing says "microchip compatible," check whether it means the implanted chip or a proprietary collar tag — these are meaningfully different products with different daily-use implications.

### What is the difference between the SureFlap standard and the Connect version?

The core microchip recognition hardware is identical. The Connect version adds a Sure Petcare Hub (sold separately) that enables iOS/Android app control, remote lock/unlock, per-pet activity logging, and push notifications for battery and lock status. The Connect has one important caveat the standard version avoids: the Hub's security updates are supported for only 2 years from purchase date. If app features aren't important to you, the standard version provides equivalent access control without the hub cost or support expiration.

### Will a smart dog door keep raccoons and wildlife out?

SureFlap doors have a 1–2 second lock delay after the flap closes, which experienced wildlife can exploit. Multiple owners in warmer climates report raccoons learning to defeat this delay. Pawport's dual steel deadbolts engage before an animal can follow through, making it the stronger wildlife deterrent. If wildlife intrusion is your primary motivation for upgrading, Pawport's hardware is worth the price difference over SureFlap.

### My dog is 40 lbs — which true microchip door fits them?

No true-microchip smart door currently on the market is reliably designed for dogs above about 25 lbs. SureFlap's largest version handles cats and small dogs, not a 40-pound dog. For larger breeds, the market gap is real — you're looking at collar-tag solutions like Pawport (no size ceiling) or PetSafe's SmartKey collar sensor system. A no-collar microchip door for large dogs simply doesn't exist commercially as of 2026.

### How long does microchip learning take on a SureFlap door?

In learning mode, the SureFlap reads your pet's chip passively as they pass through the open frame. Most dogs register within 1–3 passes. Dogs with chips that have migrated from the original implant site — common in older dogs who've gained weight — may require more attempts or produce shorter read range. You can also register chips manually via the collar tag input method: program the chip number using a temporary collar tag, then remove the tag once the chip is registered.

### Is the PetSafe SmartDoor a true microchip door for dogs?

No, and this distinction is important. PetSafe's own documentation confirms microchip recognition is available only for cats on the Medium size door. Dogs require the SmartKey collar sensor (sold separately, approximately $25). Most retailer product pages don't prominently state this. If you're a dog owner evaluating the SmartDoor specifically for implanted microchip access, go directly to PetSafe's support site to verify before ordering.

### What happens to Pawport's app features if the company shuts down?

The collar tag itself uses Bluetooth proximity detection, which doesn't require Pawport's servers to function for basic door operation. Remote scheduling, Alexa integration, and app-based control depend on their cloud infrastructure. If Pawport's servers went offline, the door would likely continue operating via local Bluetooth proximity but lose remote features. SureFlap carries a similar cloud dependency through the Connect Hub. For maximum long-term resilience against company risk, the SureFlap Standard — no app, no cloud dependency, purely local operation — is the most future-proof choice in this category.
